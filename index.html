OVERVIEW

We all want to be accepted for being fully and authentically ourselves.

Being your best self, and using your gifts and strengths in a way that creates a satisfying life, for you and those that matter to you, is the culmination of living life “on purpose.”

Exploring your thoughts about your purpose will help set you on the right path early in life. It’s pretty heavy concept, but an important one to explore.

This project will help you take the first step.

Lao Tzu, in The Art of War, said, “A journey of a thousand miles must begin with a single step”.

Trust that wherever you go, you have been working to lay the foundation for making the best of the trip.

INSTRUCTIONS

Project yourself 3 years into the future...
Using your creative imagination, see your future-self as having achieved everything you desire.
This picture is perfect! Your life is EXACTLY the way you have always wished it could be!

In the form below, describe this future picture.

START

The Ideal Version of myself in 3 years...

Include things like: The work you are doing; The hobbies you are enjoying; The passion projects you are exploring; The holidays you are taking; The lifestyle you have created; The people you are surrounded by...

Write it in first person with an active voice, as though you were relaying the story to another person right now.
Using words such as... “I am”; “I have”; “I enjoy”; “I will”... helps to achieve this.

Discover more about your future-self...

Using the description of your ideal future... answer the questions below.

PROJECT 1 My Vision Board

Using your descriptions above and your chosen Word Processing software, create a vision board of your future-self to provide you with inspiration and motivation.


INSTRUCTIONS
1. Open a new document;
2. Use the Page Setup features to create your document in LANDSCAPE;
3. Construct your vision board by inserting tables into your document;
4. Insert images and text within the table that represent your future-self;
5. Format your vision board as required for layout on a single page.
NOTE: You may need to adjust image or text sizes to assist with your layout.
